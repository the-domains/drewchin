Philip Lee Studio

Concentration: Craft // Form

Fall 2015

Professor Lee laid a strong foundation in the process of form-making and architectural representational techniques. He approached the language of architecture very carefully when speaking to the class. As students we were expected to “think with our hands” and release the sterilized “ideas” of architecture education. This allowed students to develop their own unique critical and creative methods. By restricting the materials and tools used, Lee established a fair framework to evaluate the class. The introductory project was to create an abstracted reinterpretation of Max Ernst’s “Masters Bedroom” collage. The second assignment was to design a transforming screen that creates, morphs, and/or obscures views. The final project was to design a fictional city block based on an individually chosen passage from Italo Calvino’s “Invisible Cities.”
